# Financial AML Business Expectations

## Business Process

AML investigation workflows typically include alert/case intake, transaction enrichment, party due diligence, evidence review, case analysis, recommendation, QC, and post-disposition actions.

## Decisions And Authority

The agent may draft, summarize, recommend, retrieve, and prepare analyst-facing artifacts. It must not independently execute final AML dispositions.

Human approval is required before:

- SAR narrative finalization or filing.
- Case closure.
- Client exit recommendation.
- RFI generation or external communication.
- Customer contact.
- Case-management write-back.

## Policies And SOPs

The user/team should provide AML investigation SOPs, escalation rules, SAR/RFI procedures, case-closure rules, evidence standards, typology guides, and analyst/QC responsibilities when available.

## Required Output Behavior

Outputs should be evidence-grounded, concise, analyst-facing, and explicit about missing data. Material claims should include evidence IDs or citations.

## Failure And Escalation

When evidence is missing, stale, contradictory, or outside approved sources, the agent should stop, state the gap, and request analyst review.
